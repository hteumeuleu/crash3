Scarab Beetle (continued) - When breeding, scarab beetles take great care to give their lavae the best possible chance in life by raising them on a healthy diet. Wood mulch forming on the inside of rotting logs is ideal; but, when pushed, other decomposing plant material such as leaf mulch or compost can provide healthy snacks. The best material is dung as it has a naturally warm feel.

After a period of gestation in their chosen incubation medium, the scarab lavae will reach maximum size. At this point they will make small, oval soil cocoons for themselves. They will then stay there for about a month reading the paper before hatching out as fully-fledged adult beetles ready to deal with the responsibilities of life.
