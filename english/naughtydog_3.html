Crash Bandicoot 3: Warped introduces Crash fans to the totally new flying, motorcycle and jet-ski levels. How did Naughty Dog approach these?
We didn't want to make a flight simulator, just like we didn't want to make a motorcycle simulator and we didn't want to make a jet-ski simulator. What we wanted to make was a Crash level, and a Crash level is all about seeing your enemy, knowing that if you don't do something, your enemy's going to do something to you. So you're having constant challenges. You don't need to watch the planes when they go around behind you because they're not going to come up behind you and cheaply hit you from behind. They're going to come out in front of you and they're going to give you ample time to shoot them before turning around. . . and shooting at you. So we're trying to create more of an arcade experience, more of a friendly experience than a simulator.
