Bandicoot (continued) - Bandicoots have developed very unusual feet where the second and third toes have actually grown together; many biologists attribute the animal's superior hopping abilities to these specialised feet. In the Perambulidae Thrideeus species, this adaptation has been taken further than any other, with reports of double jumping, turbo spin attacks and body slams used to deal with prey. Sadly, little empirical evidence exists of this species. . .

On the other hand, some evolutionist theories put the development of the animal's distinctive toe structure down to behavioural factors. Well, think about it. . . wouldn't repeatedly stubbing your foot on the bottom step every morning be bound to have some impact on your anatomy when applied over millions of years? Bandicoots are traditionally said to be clumsy creatures who put their feet right in it. . .
